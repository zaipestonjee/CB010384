Sigiriya(Lion Rock)

Picture of the Lion Rock

Sigiriya rock is located in Dambulla which is a 3 hour journey from the Katunayake airport.It is was declared a UNESCO site in 1982 due to its archaeological and historical significance in our country.Its history is said dated upto pre historic times with areas around Sigiriya being occupied.

History

In 477AD when king Kashyapa 1st seized the throne from his father with the assistance of the army chief,the correct heir fled to India fearful of death.Kashyapa then moved the capital and operated from the rock of Sigiriya,fearing the correct heir(Mogallana) would bring an army from India. As he moved his capital to Sigiriya,it was transformed into a palace.However after Mogallana came back and Kashyapa took his own life,Sigiriya was transformed into a Buddhist monastery complex(according to wiki).

Things to see

Sigiriya is a big rock with old paintings which are fading but with restoration projects can still be seen.These frescoes(pictures)can still be seen when climbing to the top of Sigiriya.According to holidify there appear to have been 500 frescoes on this rock but only around 22 can be seen today.Another historical item would be The Mirror Wall. Originally this wall was so polished that the king could see himself when he walked past it.However with years of decay and visitors,the wall has been scribbled on by visitors with some dating from the 8th century.Another important aspect of Sigiriya are the Water Gardens.These are said to be among the oldest advanced gardens in the World

The Sigiriya frescoes

It is recommended to proceed to climb early as 7am according to the way fares as it won't be to hot and crowded when climbing. Getting to the top,visitors are awarded a stunning view of the green surroundings.
